Super Easy Crock Pot Chili

Description

Who doesn't like chili? Perfect for cold winter nights and hot summer evenings alike, this crock-pot chili is not only fast and easy to make, but also quite nutritious. Feel free to spice it up with bell peppers, hot peppers, gourmet onions, alternative meats (here we mean lamb or pork, not people) or whatever else floats your boat.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 pounds of ground beef — you can add sausage or replace with ground chicken, turkey, or vegetarian meat crumbles if you prefer
  • 1 chopped onion — yellow onions are best for this recipe, but you can use whatever you want
  • 1 chopped green pepper — you can use other colors for variety if you like
  • 2 cloves minced garlic
  • 2 16-ounce cans of red kidney beans, rinsed and drained
  • 2 14-ounce cans of diced tomatoes; use fire-roasted tomatoes if available
  • 3 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1 tsp. cumin
  • 2 tsp. garlic salt
  • 1 tsp. black pepper
  • 1 jalapeno pepper, chopped. You may devein it if you dislike the heat from jalapenos

Steps

  1. Season the meat with garlic salt and black pepper, then cook in a skillet over medium-high heat. Drain.
  2. Add the onions, bell pepper, and garlic cloves. Cook until fragrant, 3-4 minutes.
  3. Add the meaty mix to your crock pot. Turn the heat to low.
  4. Add the beans, tomatoes, chili powder, cumin, jalapenos, and any extras you want to the pot.
  5. Stir. Cook on low for 5-6 hours or high for 3-4 hours.
  6. Serve and enjoy! Garnish with crackers, green onions, sour cream, cheddar cheese, hot sauce, or your choice of fixins.
